  6. How Amazon.com (http://www.amazon.com) and Alibaba.com (https://www.alibaba.com/) exhibit the design principles of repetition, contrast, proximity, and alignment

    Repetition: Both Amazon.com and Alibaba.com use repetition to create a cohesive and consistent design. The company logo is repeated in the upper left corner of every page, and the navigation menu is repeated across the top of every page. Additionally, the search bar and categories of products are consistently placed in the same location on each page. Both sites also use repetition to display product listings, with each item being presented in a similar format across the website.

    Contrast: Both Amazon.com and Alibaba.com use contrast to make important elements stand out. Call-to-action buttons are in a contrasting color to make them stand out from the rest of the page. Both sites also use contrasting colors and font sizes to highlight important information such as sale prices, ratings, and customer reviews.

    Proximity: Both Amazon.com and Alibaba.com use proximity to group related elements together. Both sites group related product information together, such as product images, descriptions, and customer reviews. And also products in the same category are placed together.

    Alignment: Both Amazon.com and Alibaba.com use alignment to create a sense of order and structure on their pages. For example, product listings are aligned in a grid format, with each item being the same size and shape. Both sites also use alignment to create a hierarchy of information, with important elements such as product titles and prices being aligned in a consistent manner.
